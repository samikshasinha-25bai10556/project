## 📄 Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers impersonate a trusted organization to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or OTPs.
It is commonly carried out through emails, messages, or fake websites that appear legitimate.

A typical phishing attack happens in several steps. First, the attacker sends a fake email or message that looks like it is from a bank, online service, or institution. 
The message often creates urgency, such as claiming that the user’s account will be locked or that unusual activity has been detected. 
Second, the message contains a link that directs the victim to a fake website designed to look identical to the original. 
Third, the victim is asked to enter login credentials or personal information. 
Once submitted, the attacker captures the data and uses it to access the victim’s accounts.

Students, elderly individuals, and people who are less aware of cybersecurity practices are commonly targeted because they may not recognize suspicious links or fake messages.
The consequences of phishing can be serious, including financial loss, unauthorized account access, identity theft, and misuse of personal data.

To prevent phishing attacks, users should verify the sender, avoid clicking on unknown links, and enable two-factor authentication.
Being cautious and aware is essential to staying safe online.
